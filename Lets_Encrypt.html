Let’s Encrypt

Date lun. 16 novembre 2015 Modified lun. 16 novembre 2015 Tags PKI / Certificate

Let's Encrypt, is a Certificate Authority created by Linux Fundation in 2015, which issues certificate freely and automatically.

Let's Encrypt uses the ACME (Automated Certificate Management Environment) protocol to issue certificates without any human intervention. This is accomplished by running a certificate management agent on the web server.

An automated process avoids human errors such as forgetting certificate renewal or mishandling private key.

Thanks to an automated issuance, the certificate lifetime may be reduced to 90 days. This short lifetime limits damage from key compromise and mis-issuance. Stolen keys and mis-issued certificates are valid for a shorter period of time.

Let's Encrypt is a recent certificate authority and the root certificate authority is not yet trusted in most browsers. In order to be broadly trusted right away, the intermediate certificate is also cross-signed by another certificate authority, IdenTrust. This trick introduces a weakness because the root certificate of IdenTrust is signed with SHA1 algorithm.
